A galaxy's structure is not single-component. With sufficient depth and
resolution one can decompose it into a central bulge plus an extended disk —
two physically distinct ingredients with their own size–mass relations and
their own growth histories.

The third chapter of my thesis extends the CLAUDS+HSC pipeline to
**multi-component Sérsic fitting** in the UV and the optical simultaneously.
The result: star-forming galaxies show clear inside-out disk growth, with the
bulge component largely fixed; quiescent galaxies grow at the bulge level,
with the disk diminishing in relative weight. The size–mass relation people
have been measuring for decades is a flux-weighted convolution of two
underlying tracks.
