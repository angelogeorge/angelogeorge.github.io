[KiloGAS](https://kilogas.org/) is a kilo-galaxy integral-field-spectroscopy
survey designed to capture the *spatial signatures* of star-formation
quenching in nearby galaxies. Whereas my PhD focused on integrated
properties of distant galaxies, KiloGAS lets me ask where in a galaxy the
quenching first takes hold — the centre, the outskirts, or in patchy regions.

At ASIAA I'm working on combining KiloGAS spatially-resolved star-formation
maps with structural information from imaging to ask whether a galaxy's
*structural* state predicts the *spatial pattern* of its quenching. The
goal is to bridge the morphological-quenching and environmental-quenching
literatures with a single unified diagnostic.

Project at ASIAA · 2025–present.
