Massive quiescent galaxies grow in size after they stop forming stars — but
*how* they grow is debated. Two main hypotheses: minor mergers that build an
extended low-density envelope, or new arrivals (recently-quenched larger
galaxies dropping into the quiescent population) inflating the median size
without any individual galaxy growing.

Using the same CLAUDS+HSC pipeline I built during my PhD, I compared
quiescent galaxies in the cores of massive clusters (HSC-SSP cluster
catalogues) with field galaxies at matched mass and redshift. The result, in
ApJ 2025: in cluster cores the size growth is **slower** in the rest-frame
UV than in the field, consistent with a halt in the accretion of low-mass
companions once a galaxy enters a dense environment. The optical sizes
agree between environments, telling us the cluster signal is in the youngest
stellar populations.

Published in [ApJ 987, 45 (2025)](https://doi.org/10.3847/1538-4357/addc6b).
